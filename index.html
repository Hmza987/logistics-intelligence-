⚠ DATA LIMITATION — DARK FLEET
IMF PortWatch counts only AIS-transmitting vessels. Due to GPS jamming and IRGC-directed AIS-off protocols, a significant share of transits are invisible to AIS tracking. Windward AI imagery (May 11) detected 9 transits and 21 dark tankers at anchor in Iranian waters — roughly 3–4× the IMF AIS count for the same period. The IRGC Navy separately claimed 31–35 vessels/day on May 22–23 (Bernama), but this figure is unverified self-reporting and cannot be independently reconciled with AIS or imagery data. True daily transits are likely between the IMF floor (~2–6) and the IRGC ceiling (~35), with the most credible independent estimate around 9–15/day.

⚠ Key Model Limitation Identified
The linear β₂ (capacity utilisation) term systematically under-predicts peak-crisis rates by 15–25%. Real markets exhibit a convex / near-logistic rate curve as utilisation approaches 95–100% — the linear approximation diverges sharply at those extremes. Kalouptsidi (2014, AER) documents this non-linearity in bulk shipping; the same dynamic applies to container markets. A log-linear specification or a logistic utilisation term would reduce peak RMSE from ~14% to an estimated ~6–8%.
Literature: Notteboom & Vernimmen (2009) J.Transport Geography (bunker β) • Kalouptsidi (2014) AER (capacity elasticity) • IMF WP/24/058 “Sailing into Headwinds: The Red Sea Disruption” (detour & war-risk) • Drewry Port Performance Index 2021–22 (congestion pass-through) • Hummels & Schaur (2013) AER (time-value in trade)
